# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in the Pandoras Box project
a welcoming, respectful, and productive experience for everyone, regardless of age, background,
experience level, education, nationality, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, disability,
personal appearance, religion, or sexual orientation.

We pledge to act in ways that contribute to an open, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behaviour that contributes to a positive environment:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Accepting constructive feedback graciously
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other contributors

Examples of unacceptable behaviour:

- Disrespectful, demeaning, or exclusionary remarks directed at individuals
- Publishing private information about others without permission
- Sustained disruption of discussions or project work
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered unprofessional in a collaborative setting

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying acceptable behaviour standards and taking
fair, proportionate corrective action in response to any conduct they consider inappropriate.

Maintainers have the right to remove, edit, or reject contributions (comments, commits, issues,
and other interactions) that do not align with this Code of Conduct.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces -- GitHub repositories, issue trackers,
discussion boards, and any other venue where community members represent the project.

It also applies when an individual is representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behaviour may be reported to the project maintainers by opening a
report through GitHub:

- For sensitive reports, use a **private security advisory**: the repository's **Security ->
  Report a vulnerability** form (visible to maintainers only), or
- For non-sensitive matters, open an issue or a **GitHub Discussion**.

All reports will be reviewed promptly and treated with discretion. Maintainers will respect
the privacy of those reporting incidents.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will follow these guidelines in determining consequences:

### 1. Correction

**Impact:** A single lapse in professional conduct.

**Consequence:** A private written notice explaining the concern and expected behaviour going
forward. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Impact:** A pattern of behaviour or a more significant single incident.

**Consequence:** A formal warning with specified conditions for continued participation.
Continued violations may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Impact:** A serious violation or continued behaviour after warnings.

**Consequence:** Temporary suspension from all project participation. No interaction with
project spaces during this period. Violation of this ban may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Impact:** A pattern of sustained violations, severe conduct, or targeted mistreatment of
community members.

**Consequence:** Permanent removal from all project participation.
